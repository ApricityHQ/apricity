# Funding & Runway

## Use of Funds

**How will you allocate the next round?**

Porch is raising a **$5.5M Seed extension / Pre-Series A round** to scale from one pilot metro to a repeatable multi-city operating model over 18 months.

| Category | Amount | % of Round | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product & Engineering | $2.0M | 36% | Build vendor tooling, routing intelligence, and customer retention loops |
| Market Operations | $1.4M | 25% | City launch teams, vendor onboarding, QA for fulfillment consistency |
| Sales & Marketing | $1.0M | 18% | City-level demand generation, referrals, lifecycle marketing |
| Logistics Partnerships | $0.5M | 9% | Courier integrations, service-level guarantees, launch incentives |
| G&A (legal, finance, people ops) | $0.3M | 5% | Compliance, contracts, accounting, hiring infrastructure |
| Buffer / Contingency | $0.3M | 5% | Runway protection against launch variance |

**What are the key hires?**

Priority 1 (first 6 months):
- Head of Operations (multi-city rollout)
- Senior Full-Stack Engineer (vendor + customer workflows)
- Growth Marketing Lead
- City Operations Managers (2)

Priority 2 (months 6–12):
- Routing/Logistics Engineer
- Vendor Success Lead
- Data Analyst (retention + demand forecasting)
- Finance Manager (part-time/full-time transition)

Target end-state after deployment period: **~18 FTE** (from ~7 today).

**What are the product milestones?**

1. **Vendor Console v2 (Q3):** Faster weekly inventory upload, substitutions, and auto-carryover from prior week.
2. **Checkout & Delivery Reliability (Q3–Q4):** Delivery window confidence and proactive substitution controls.
3. **Household Reorder System (Q4):** One-click reorder, preferences, and seasonal recommendations.
4. **Multi-City Launch Playbook (Q1 next year):** Repeatable vendor onboarding and launch operations kit.
5. **Unit-Economics Dashboard (Q1 next year):** Cohort-level contribution margin visibility by market and route density.

Working assumption: **~$0.9M** cash at close (subject to final close timing and pilot spend).

### Runway

- At current burn: **~6 months** without raise.
- Post-close with planned ramp: **~18–20 months** runway.

### Revenue Model

Porch monetizes through a blended transaction model:
1. **Marketplace take rate** on GMV (primary).
2. **Customer delivery/service fees** (secondary).
3. **Future membership layer** for higher-frequency households.

### Pricing (Current / Planned)

| Item | Approach |
|---|---|
| Vendor payout | ~80% of order subtotal to vendor |
| Platform take | ~20% of order subtotal |
| Delivery/service fee | Zone/time-window based |
| Optional membership (future) | Lower variable fees + loyalty benefits |

### 18-Month GMV / Revenue Trajectory (Base Case)

| Period | Monthly GMV | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Today | $40K–$70K | Pilot-stage variability |
| +6 months | $140K–$200K | Metro #2 launched |
| +12 months | $250K–$350K | Retention loops maturing |
| +18 months | $500K+ | Multi-city repeatability threshold |

### Forecast Assumptions

1. Active household growth driven by referral + local channel partnerships.
2. Vendor retention remains strong due to favorable economics and low workflow burden.
3. Repeat purchase rate improves as reorder UX and reliability improve.
4. Delivery economics improve with route density and batching.
5. No heavy capex required (asset-light logistics strategy).

### Scenario Planning

**Bear case:** slower city launch velocity and lower reorder frequency delay Series A timing by 2–3 quarters.

**Base case:** three active metros, stable contribution profile, and Series A readiness on schedule.

**Bull case:** stronger-than-expected vendor density and referral loops accelerate GMV to $700K+ monthly by end of period.

### Break-Even Discussion

Porch is not optimizing for full operating break-even during this financing window. The objective is to prove repeatable, contribution-positive expansion and a defensible local supply network.

### Key Financial Risks

1. **Route-density risk:** underutilized delivery routes compress contribution margin.
2. **Launch complexity risk:** inconsistent vendor onboarding quality can impact fulfillment reliability.
3. **Demand concentration risk:** over-reliance on a few neighborhoods or vendor anchors.
4. **Competitive response risk:** local-market promotions by large grocery delivery incumbents.
